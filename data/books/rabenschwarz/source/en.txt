# Chapter 1: Africa

The sun rose as it did every morning. But today, something was different.

“Good morning, little mouse,” Klara’s mother called as she pulled the curtains open with a sharp tug.

“Mum, don’t call me that!” Klara grumbled.

She quickly got dressed and ran into the kitchen.

“Wow,” she murmured when she saw the breakfast table. There were pancakes with maple syrup and fresh raspberries.

She immediately sat down and stirred her hot chocolate—with a spoon, of course.

Halfway through breakfast, she asked, “Why are you making me a breakfast like this on a weekday? What are you trying to tell me?”

Her parents exchanged a worried glance.

Finally, her father began. “Sweetheart, your mother and I have to go to Africa, and—”

“That’s great! I’ve always wanted to go to Africa,” Klara interrupted.

Then her mother stepped in. “Klara, we would have taken you with us, but we’re building the hospital in a very remote place. There isn’t a school there.”

Tears filled Klara’s eyes.

“Where am I supposed to go?” she asked, trying to hold back a sob.

“You’ll stay at a very nice boarding school until we return home in six months,” her mother said soothingly.

It did not help.

“Which boarding school?” Klara asked, beginning to panic.

“Rabenschwarz Castle Boarding School,” her father replied.

Klara stood up and ran away. A short while later, she came back and took her hot chocolate with her.

An hour later, her mother entered her room.

“Get ready. You have to go to school.”

“No. I’m staying home,” Klara growled.

“But Klara, don’t you want to say goodbye to your friends?”

“No, no, and no again!” Klara shouted.

“All right. You may stay home today as an exception,” her mother said, slightly intimidated.

Klara could no longer hold back her tears and began to cry.

# Chapter 2: The Boarding School

One week later, Klara packed her belongings.

“Are you ready?” her mother asked, poking her head through the gap in the door.

Klara nodded.

Half an hour later, the entire Schmidt family—that was Klara’s family name—was sitting in the car on the way to the boarding school.

After what felt like an eternity, a very large castle appeared in front of them.

The castle had three towers. At first glance, no one would have guessed that this was Rabenschwarz Castle. It was built from pale sandstone that would have glittered in the sunlight—had the sun been shining.

Instead, it was raining extremely heavily.

Inside, the castle looked almost intimidating. Everything was large, spacious, and bright.

“Hello, do you happen to know where the headmistress’s office is?” Klara’s father asked a girl who was walking past.

The girl had straight, waist-length strawberry-blonde hair, brown doe-like eyes, and countless freckles.

“Of course. This way,” the girl answered.

“I’m Charly, by the way,” she added, turning towards Klara.

After a few steps, she turned around.

“Sorry, wrong direction. It really is this way,” she mumbled.

“Isn’t she nice?” Klara’s mother whispered.

Klara merely gave a brief nod.

Charly stopped in front of a door.

“Thank you,” Klara’s mother said.

“You’re welcome,” Charly replied.

Klara’s father knocked.

“Come in,” said a very calm voice.

He opened the door.

An elegant woman was sitting inside. She had glossy black hair, which she wore in a fishtail braid that kept it away from her face. Her eyes seemed to glow even though they were pitch-black.

“Ah, the Schmidt family. I’ve been expecting you. Please, sit down,” she said.

The Schmidt family took their seats.

“My name is Katharina Elisabeth von Rabenschwarz,” she continued. “Welcome to Rabenschwarz Castle, Klara. The rules at this school are no different from those at any other school. Your roommate is Charlotte Clasen. She will accompany you during your first few days until you know your way around.”

A moment later, Mrs von Rabenschwarz spoke into something resembling a microphone.

“Charlotte Clasen, please report to the headmistress’s office.”

“What did I do?” asked the strawberry-blonde girl as soon as she entered.

She had even forgotten to knock. Panic was written all over her face.

Mrs von Rabenschwarz looked at Charly with an almost amused expression.

“You haven’t done anything. This is Klara Schmidt. She will be your roommate for the time being. I would like you to accompany her, answer all her questions, and make sure she always feels welcome here. Understood?”

Charly nodded.

After Mrs von Rabenschwarz had listed and explained all the school rules, she warned Klara not to break any of them.

About fifteen minutes later, the Schmidt family was standing outside the office again.

“Shall we take you to your room?” Klara’s mother asked.

Klara shook her head firmly and pulled the suitcase and large travel bag from her father’s hands.

She briefly hugged her parents and followed Charly down the corridor.

A short while later, they stood in front of a large, dark wooden door with a golden doorknob.

Charly turned the knob and opened the door.

# Chapter 3: Arrival in the Room

Klara could hardly believe it.

She had always imagined that boarding-school rooms would be boring or uncomfortable, but this room was neither.

It was large, with white plastered walls. There was a bed in each of two corners, but they were not ordinary beds. They were four-poster beds with pale-green bedding and delicate white curtains.

Matching curtains hung over the arched windows above the beds and above a large desk.

The desk was made from dark wood and was large enough for Charly and Klara to sit at it together.

A huge wardrobe, also made from dark wood, stood against one wall. The floor consisted of old wooden boards that occasionally creaked.

Opposite the wardrobe was a door leading into a bathroom. Klara guessed that the bathroom had been added later.

Inside were a sink, a toilet, and a small bathtub.

But Klara’s attention was drawn to the window and the beautiful landscape beyond it.

There was a gigantic meadow covered in green grass, with a large forest of dark-green leaves behind it.

She hurried to the window, threw it open, and enjoyed the fresh air.

By now, the rain had stopped, and the sky was glowing in the most beautiful shade of blue.

Klara only wished her mood could change just as quickly.

“The view is beautiful, isn’t it?” Charly asked, as though she had read Klara’s thoughts.

Klara merely nodded and became lost in thought.

The ringing of a bell suddenly startled her.

“That’s the lunch bell. Are you coming? We’re having cabbage casserole today, with ice cream for dessert,” Charly called, already licking her lips in anticipation.

But Klara shook her head. She was not hungry.

Charly went to lunch alone while Klara began unpacking.

Some time later, Charly returned and helped her unpack the rest of her things. Afterwards, both girls collapsed exhausted onto Klara’s bed.

“Oh, I almost forgot. Mrs von Rabenschwarz has excused us from lessons tomorrow so I can show you everything,” Charly said. “And this is for you.”

She stood up, opened a drawer in the large wardrobe, and took out a school uniform.

She handed it to Klara.

Klara changed and examined herself in the mirror.

The school uniform consisted of highly polished black strap shoes, black tights, a black skirt with a green checked pattern, a white shirt, and a grey blazer with the Rabenschwarz Castle crest on its chest.

The crest showed a raven against a pale-blue background, holding a four-leaf clover in its beak.

Klara’s gaze moved upwards and settled on her emerald-green eyes.

She untied the hair she had worn in a ponytail all day. Her shoulder-length, slightly wavy chestnut-brown hair fell across her face.

She looked over at Charly, who was already sitting on her bed reading a book.

Then, without looking up, Charly said, “You only have to wear the school uniform during lessons.”

“Thank you.”

“Why are you thanking me?”

“Because you told me something useful.”

“Oh.”

Charly grinned and began talking wildly about the teachers, the lessons, and even the food at Rabenschwarz Castle.

Klara began to feel a little better.

# Chapter 4: A New Acquaintance at Supper

Klara felt well enough to go to supper.

Eventually, at least. Charly had led her in the wrong direction twice.

“My brother always says I’m scatterbrained. He isn’t entirely wrong,” Charly said, sounding slightly dejected.

Klara felt a little sorry for her.

They sat down at one of the two long tables.

The tables were covered with all kinds of food: bread, cheese, salami, ham, butter, and much more.

Charly immediately poured herself a glass of water and drank it in one gulp.

Klara could not help smiling again.

Then her gaze settled on the girl sitting diagonally across from her.

Klara stared at her in surprise. Her gaze moved to the headmistress, then back to the girl.

The resemblance was remarkable.

The girl had exactly the same glowing, pitch-black eyes and the same glossy, raven-black hair. But she wore hers loose, and it reached down to her elbows.

The girl briefly looked back at Klara, but only for a second.

After the meal, she approached Klara and Charly.

“I’m Constanze von Rabenschwarz, but everyone calls me Conny.”

“You’re a von Rabenschwarz?” Klara asked, impressed.

Charly was unable to say a word.

“Yes. The headmistress is my aunt. My mother and she are identical twins. But I have to go back to my room now. I’ll tell you more another time, if you’d like.”

“Yeeees!” Charly shouted after her.

Later, the room grew quiet as darkness spread across the forest, the meadow, the small town at the foot of the castle, and the entire castle itself.

# Chapter 5: The Night-Time Excursion

Klara came out of the bathroom.

Charly was already in bed reading a book. It was something about a guardian of nature, or something like that.

Klara climbed into bed and stared at the ceiling for a while.

Suddenly, she noticed movement beside her and jerked her head to the side.

It was only Charly putting down her book and switching off her bedside lamp.

Klara tossed and turned restlessly. She could not sleep.

After what must have been an hour, she gave up and got out of bed.

Charly had fallen asleep long ago.

Klara tiptoed to the window and looked outside.

She no longer felt good. The positive feeling she had built up throughout the afternoon had vanished.

She wondered what her parents were doing. Were they still at home? Were they travelling to Africa? Or were they already there?

Without thinking much about it, she put on her shoes and crept out of the room and into the corridor.

She sneaked down the great staircase, went through the door, and stepped onto the large meadow.

Before she knew what was happening, she began to run.

She did not stop until she reached a small hill covered in plants.

The castle towered behind her and looked almost threatening at night, but she paid no attention to it.

She simply sank to the ground, buried her face in her hands, and cried.

After a while—a very long while—her eyes seemed unable to produce any more tears.

She stood up and walked back towards the castle.

When she reached the room, she quickly washed her face and returned to bed.

The night-time excursion had not truly made her feel better, but it had made her tired, so she fell asleep without difficulty.

As the saying goes, “The world looks different in the morning.”

# Chapter 6: The First Morning

Klara slowly woke up.

The moment she opened her eyes, she became frightened and screamed.

A red-haired, freckled creature was dangling directly above her face.

“Good morning, sleepyhead! Get up, or we’ll be late for breakfast—and believe me, you don’t want to miss breakfast!” Charly shouted more loudly than necessary.

“Give me five minutes,” Klara said sleepily.

She got up and shuffled into the bathroom.

About seven minutes later, Charly and Klara were on their way to the dining hall.

Klara could hardly believe her eyes. Charly had not been lying.

There was fruit salad, toast, jam, bread rolls, cereal, porridge, hot chocolate, tea, scrambled eggs, and eggs prepared in almost every possible way.

Klara sat down beside Charly in the same place as the previous evening, and both girls immediately began to eat.

Charly took some fruit salad, toast, and jam. Then she stopped and handed Klara the plate of toast.

Klara smiled and took a slice.

Charly gave Klara a cup and took one for herself. Then she pulled a jug towards them and held it out.

“Here. This is the best hot chocolate in Germany.”

Klara poured herself some and carefully took a sip.

She froze.

This was not only the best hot chocolate in Germany. It was the best hot chocolate in all of Europe.

Charly began speaking with her mouth full.

“So, first we can explore the school. Then we’ll visit the school grounds, and after that I’ll show you the town. What do you think? Oh, and we can go to my house.”

Klara looked at her in surprise.

“I thought you lived at the boarding school.”

“Yes, of course.”

“But isn’t your home far away, like mine?”

“No, my home is here in town.”

“Oh.”

Klara took another sip of hot chocolate.

A while later, once everyone had finished eating, Mrs von Rabenschwarz stood up, and every pair of eyes turned towards her.

“My dear pupils, I hope you all enjoyed your breakfast and slept well,” she said.

She spoke at an ordinary volume, yet everyone could understand every word. She had a presence that immediately filled the entire room.

She spoke about the upcoming activities and other school matters.

“And now I would like you all to return to your rooms and prepare for the school day,” she concluded.

Charly pulled Klara to her feet.

“Come on!”

As they left the dining hall, Mrs von Rabenschwarz stopped them.

“You do remember that I have excused you from lessons today, don’t you?”

“Yes, of course,” Charly answered immediately.

“Good. Off you go, then. Have fun.”

# Chapter 7: A Harbor for Worlds

“And this is our classroom, and that is the ninth-grade classroom.”

It had been going on like this for half an hour.

Charly talked like a waterfall.

“And now we’re coming to what I think is the best place in the entire school building,” she said excitedly as she turned a corner. “This is one place I can always find.”

Klara stared in amazement when Charly opened the large double doors.

It was a library—the most beautiful library Klara had ever seen.

The floor was made from dark wooden boards. The shelves were made from pale wood and overflowing with books.

There were several floors connected by spiral staircases, and large ladders helped readers reach the higher shelves.

Above every bookcase was a small sign written in elegant, curling handwriting, explaining what kind of books could be found there.

“History Books,” one sign read.

“Fantasy,” said another.

“Crime Stories,” Klara read on a third sign.

She hurried towards it, with Charly close behind her.

Klara immediately pulled out a book with *Sherlock Holmes* written on the cover.

She put it back.

As she looked along the shelf, she spotted the fifth volume of her favourite series, *The Schlei Detectives*.

She was currently reading the fourth volume.

Perhaps she would not have to ask her grandparents to send her the remaining books after all—or search for them herself in a shop and buy them.
